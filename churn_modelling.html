Project Description

Objective

The aim of this project is to predict if a customer of the bank decides to terminate his or her bank membership or not, by applying logistic regression and artificial neural networks. This dataset, taken from Kaggle, contains details of a bank's customers, with one column being a binary variable reflecting if the customer closed his or her account.

Methodology

I began this project by cleaning the data as well as performing exploratory data analysis (EDA). Next, I performed one-hot encoding, which is the process of converting categorical data into numerical data. In this case, we had 'Geography' and 'Gender' as categorical variables, hence they have to be one-hot encoded.

Afterwards, I plotted a correlation plot of the customer features against the likelihood of the customer leaving the bank. The correlation plot shows that the top three factors affecting customer churn is the customer's age, if the customer lives in Germany, and if the customer is an active member of the bank.

Then, I split the data into training and test sets, performed Logistic Regression as well as binary classification with Artifical Neural Networks.

Conclusion

The F1 score when using Artifical Neural Networks was 0.75, whereas it was 0.59 when Logistic Regression was used.
